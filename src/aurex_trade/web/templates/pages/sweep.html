Parameter Sweep

Step 2 of 3 — What am I doing here?

You've confirmed the strategy works in principle (Step 1). Now instead of manually guessing which settings are best, let the computer try every combination and rank them for you.

What to do: Select a strategy and set the range of values to try for each parameter (start, end, step). The sweep tries every pairing and shows you which scored highest.

Reading the results: The table shows every combination ranked by your chosen metric (Sharpe Ratio by default). The top row is the "winner" — but be cautious. Just because settings scored highest on past data doesn't guarantee they'll keep working. That's what Step 3 is for.

Tips:

  • Start with wide ranges and big steps (fast exploration), then narrow in on promising areas
  • If many nearby settings all score well, that's a good sign — the strategy isn't fragile
  • If only one specific setting works and its neighbours are terrible, be suspicious

Found your best settings? Take them to Walk-Forward Validation to check they're not a fluke.
