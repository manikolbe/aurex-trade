Backtest

Step 1 of 3 — What am I doing here?

This is your first sanity check. You're replaying past price data and pretending you were trading — did the strategy make money, or lose it? If it can't work on old data, it definitely won't work on new data.

What to do: Pick a symbol (e.g. Gold), set your Short and Long Window numbers, and hit Run. The results will tell you whether this combination of settings is worth exploring further.

How the strategy works: The SMA Crossover watches two averages of recent prices — a "fast" one (Short Window, e.g. last 10 prices) and a "slow" one (Long Window, e.g. last 30 prices). When the fast average rises above the slow one, prices are gaining momentum → buy. When it drops below → sell. Smaller numbers react quicker but produce more false signals; larger numbers are smoother but slower to respond.

Reading the results:

  • Total P&L — did it make or lose money overall?
  • Win Rate — what % of trades were profitable?
  • Sharpe Ratio — how good is the return relative to the risk? (above 1 = decent, above 2 = strong)
  • Max Drawdown — the worst drop from a high point (how scary would it have been to hold on?)
