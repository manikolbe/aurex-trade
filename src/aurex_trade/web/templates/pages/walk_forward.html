Walk-Forward Validation

Step 3 of 3 — What am I doing here?

This is the final check before you trust a strategy with real money. The sweep (Step 2) found settings that scored best on past data — but were they genuinely good, or just lucky? Walk-forward answers this by testing on data the strategy has never seen.

How it works: Your price history gets split into alternating chunks. For each pair: the first chunk ("Learning Period") is used to find the best settings, then those settings are tested on the next chunk ("Exam Period") — data the strategy has never seen before. This repeats across multiple pairs.

What to do: Use the same parameter ranges from your sweep. Set the Learning and Exam period sizes (default 7200 bars = ~1 week of minute data). Hit Run.

Reading the results:

  • If most exam periods are profitable → the strategy is robust, not a fluke
  • If performance is wildly inconsistent between exam periods → the settings are fragile, go back to Step 2 and try different ranges
  • Look at which settings get chosen each period — if the same ones keep winning, that's a strong signal

Passes walk-forward? You've found a strategy and settings you can have confidence in. Consider moving to live paper trading via the Bot page.
